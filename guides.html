Cleaning your games!

In this guide we will show you how to properly clean a game cartridge, remember DO NOT use alcohol and a Q-Tip, that's for sissies.

Tumble and Dry

Got dirty contacts on your cartridge? Throw it in with the laundry, and watch those contacts come out sparkling clean.

Sand it down

My daddy said every man needs a belt sander, this is the fastest most expedited and dangerous way to clean your cartridge.

  1. In order to open up the cartridge you should use a phillips head screwdriver, if they've got security bits drill the screws out.
  2. Remove the PCB from the plastic shell.
  3. Turn on the belt sander.
  4. Apply PCB contacts to belt sander, and watch that grime get sanded right off, and admire the sparkling contacts.
  5. If you drilled out your 3.8mm security screws do not fear, Hot Glue Gun to the Rescue! The contacts are now so clean you won't need to open it again.

Pressure Wash

Much like the Belt Sander the over the top industrial strength of a pressure washer is pretty awesome. It's pretty simple really, just place your game on the ground, and chase it around with the pressure washer after about a good 10 minutes of this the game should be clean.

Blow it!!(Duh!)

Blowing on cartridges gets saliva on them, and saliva is technically a solvent, because it is mostly water. Contrary to what collectors will tell you saliva DOES NOT stick to the contacts and dry making it dirtier, leaving a disgusting black residue from the buildup of loogie.
